Part 2: Analysis of the Results

Exercise 2 - Gas-Phase Reaction

The next step is to look at the QM energies of the reaction from chorismate to prephenate in the gas-phase.

The energies should already be present in energies.txt, and thus loaded into the spreadsheet in the previous step.

IF you are interested, the energies were calculated in Part 1, exercise 2, and are in qmonly_ener.log. Note that in this case, the energies will be printed in the correct order (so from -1.8, through 0.0, up to +1.8)

You can extract the minimised QM energy of the reaction, either by using grep; 

grep "ENER QUANTM>" qmonly_ener.log
or by extracting values manually.

Once the values are in your spreadsheet, calculate the relative energies (as you did in exercise 1). Plot the relative energies on the same graph that you produced in exercise 1. Calculate the stabilisation energy (relative QM gas-phase energy from this step minus relative QM/MM energy from exercise 1). The stabilisation energy is the difference between the reaction energy for the gas-phase reaction and the reaction energy for the enzyme catalysed reaction. The stabilisation energy shows by how much the enzyme is stabilising the reaction, and which parts of the reaction are being stabilised.

Plot the stabilisation energy against the reaction coordinate. Does your graph show support for the hypothesis that chorismate mutase catalyses the reaction by stabilising the transition state?
